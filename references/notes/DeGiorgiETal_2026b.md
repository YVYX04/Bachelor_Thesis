## 1. Introduction

In the introduction, De Giorgi et al. (2026) discusses the link between the cumulative prospect theory (CPT) of Kahneman and Tversky (1979) to illustrate the importance of the *reference point* to determine the investment psychology of retail investors. The authors use an example derived from Kahneman and Tversky (1992).

![](/assets/images/reference_point.png)

For instance, if B is the risk-free return and the initial reference point, and the investor realizes a gain–thus ending up in C, the investor can loose up to $C - B$ (if B remains the reference point) and still remain in the "gain" zone. This could lead to increased risk-taking: the so-called *house money effect* (the tendency for people to take more risk after they have already made gains. They behave as if the profit is not really their own money, but "extra" money that can be gambled more freely.)

The goal of De Giorgi et al. (2026) research is to explain momentum through a model based on the cumulative prospect theory–specifically to focus on the so-called *aspiration-level reference point* updated based on the stock's performance.
